Section 03

All Issues — Detailed Breakdown

Every identified issue with category, priority, and a specific actionable fix.

01
🚨 Malicious Spam Link Injection in Homepage Code
Source code contains 8+ injected gambling links ("slot gacor"). These are hidden from users but visible to Google, causing SEO blacklisting and security breaches.
Delete all injected links, change all CMS/admin passwords, scan for malware, and update all CMS plugins immediately.
Critical
02
🚨 Personal Volunteer Email Addresses Publicly Exposed
Three volunteers' personal Gmail IDs are shown on the public Volunteers page, exposing them to spam and phishing attacks.
Remove all personal emails. Replace with a single contact form or a unified NGO alias like volunteer@inamigosfoundation.org.in.
Critical
03
🚨 Footer Social Links Point to Wrong/Generic Websites
Twitter links to twitter.com, the "G" icon links to google.com, Pinterest links to pinterest.com — none go to InAmigos profiles. LinkedIn is absent entirely.
Replace all links with correct InAmigos social profile URLs. Add LinkedIn. Remove icons for unused platforms.
Critical
04
⚠️ Hero Slider Has No Real NGO Photos
The homepage hero only shows text on a plain dark background. For an NGO, real human photos are the #1 trust-building element — visitors form opinions in under 3 seconds.
Add high-quality photos from actual InAmigos events to each hero slide. Image + headline + CTA button is the proven format.
High
05
⚠️ "28 States" Stat Is Factually Incorrect
The stats bar shows "28 States" but the About page says InAmigos has 28 volunteers working across ~5 states. This factual inconsistency damages credibility.
Change label to "28+ Volunteers" and verify all statistics are consistent across all pages.
High
06
⚠️ Project Vikas Missing from the Causes Section
The Causes section lists only 5 projects. Project Vikas (Youth Skill Development) is described on the About page but never appears in Causes — creating an incomplete listing.
Add Project Vikas as the 6th cause card with an appropriate icon, description, and any fundraising data.
High
07
⚠️ Events Section Shows Outdated 2025 Content Only
Events from Feb–March 2025 (World Water Day, International Day of Happiness) are still prominently displayed with no upcoming events listed — making the org appear inactive.
Archive old events under "Past Events". Add upcoming events or a "Stay tuned" notice with a newsletter signup.
High
08
⚠️ LinkedIn Not Linked Anywhere on Website
InAmigos actively recruits interns and engages professionals on LinkedIn, but no LinkedIn link exists anywhere on the website — not in the header bar or footer social icons.
Add the InAmigos LinkedIn profile URL to both the top social bar and the footer social icons.
High
09
💡 About Section Is a Dense Wall of Text
The "Get to Know Us Better" section is one long paragraph with no subheadings, icons, bullet points or visual rhythm. Most visitors skip large unbroken text blocks.
Break into sections: Mission Statement, Certifications (with badge icons), Projects (bullet list), and a "Meet the Founder" photo block.
Medium
10
💡 No Testimonials or Impact Stories
There are no quotes, testimonials, or real stories from beneficiaries, volunteers, or community members — the most emotionally compelling content type for an NGO.
Add a "Voices of Change" section with 3–5 testimonials: photo, name, role, and a short quote about their InAmigos experience.
Medium
11
💡 No Donation Progress Bars or Fund Transparency
Donation buttons exist but there's no visible fundraising goal, progress bar, or fund allocation breakdown — all proven to significantly increase donor trust and conversion.
Add a progress bar per project (e.g., "₹35,000 raised of ₹1,00,000 goal") and a simple pie chart showing fund allocation.
Medium
12
💡 No Mobile Hamburger Menu
On mobile screens the full navigation bar is not accessible. Most Indian NGO visitors are on mobile — missing navigation means users cannot find Causes, Gallery, or Contact pages.
Add a responsive hamburger (☰) menu that opens a drawer with all navigation links on screens below 768px.
Medium
13
✨ Generic Visual Design with No Brand Identity
The site uses a stock CMS template with no distinctive colours, typography, or visual personality. It looks like thousands of other websites and doesn't reflect InAmigos' warmth and energy.
Define a brand palette (saffron, forest green, cream) and apply consistently. Use a distinctive serif display font. Add custom project icons.
Low
14
✨ No Email Newsletter / Subscriber List
There's no email signup anywhere. An email list enables direct communication with donors, volunteers, and supporters — the highest-ROI channel for NGOs.
Add a simple "Stay Updated" email form connected to Mailchimp (free plan). Target: "Join 500+ InAmigos supporters."
Low
