Section 03

Detailed Issue Breakdown

Each identified issue is categorised by priority, with a description of the problem and a specific, actionable fix.

01
🚨 Spam / Malicious Link Injection in Website Code
The homepage source code contains multiple injected "slot gacor" gambling links hidden in the HTML. This is a serious security breach — these links harm the website's Google ranking (SEO), violate Google's policies, and can lead to the site being blacklisted by browsers.
Remove all injected links immediately, change all admin/CMS passwords, scan the server for malware, and update the CMS and all plugins to the latest version.
Critical
02
🚨 Broken & Incorrect Footer Social Media Links
The social links in the footer point to generic URLs (twitter.com, google.com, pinterest.com) instead of the actual InAmigos social media profiles. This destroys credibility and wastes an important trust-building opportunity.
Replace all social links with the correct profile URLs: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and YouTube. Remove placeholder links for platforms not actively used.
Critical
03
🚨 Volunteer Personal Email Addresses Exposed Publicly
The Volunteers page displays personal Gmail addresses (e.g., kfaiz3662@gmail.com) publicly. This exposes volunteers to spam, phishing attacks, and privacy violations without their informed consent.
Remove personal email addresses from the public page. Replace with a single contact form or a dedicated NGO email alias (e.g., volunteer@inamigosfoundation.org.in).
Critical
04
⚠️ Hero Slider Has No Real Images or Visuals
The homepage hero section only shows text on a plain background with no photographs of actual NGO work. For an NGO, real impact images are the most powerful trust-building element. Visitors form first impressions in under 3 seconds.
Add high-quality photos from actual InAmigos events (Seva drives, Bachpanshala sessions, Prakriti drives) to the hero slider. Each slide should have a compelling image + short headline + CTA button.
High
05
⚠️ Incorrect Statistic — "28 States" Instead of "28 Volunteers"
The stats section shows "28 States" as a key figure, but the website's own About page mentions "28 volunteers." This inconsistency confuses visitors and damages credibility. InAmigos operates in about 5 states, not 28.
Correct the stat to "28+ Volunteers" or update with accurate data. Ensure all statistics across all pages are consistent and verified.
High
06
⚠️ Project Vikas Missing from the Causes / Projects Section
The homepage Causes section only lists 5 projects. Project Vikas (Youth Skill Development) is described in the About section but not shown in the Causes list, creating an incomplete picture of InAmigos' work.
Add Project Vikas as the 6th cause card in the Causes section, with a matching icon, description, and progress bar (if applicable).
High
07
⚠️ No LinkedIn Link in Navigation or Social Icons
InAmigos has an active LinkedIn presence used for intern recruitment and professional outreach, but there is no LinkedIn link anywhere on the website — not in the header, footer, or social icons.
Add a LinkedIn profile link to the social icons in both the top bar and the footer. This is especially important for attracting interns and corporate CSR partners.
High
08
⚠️ Events Section Shows Outdated Events (Feb–March 2025)
The events section prominently displays old events from early 2025 with no upcoming events listed. This makes the organisation appear inactive and reduces visitor engagement significantly.
Either add upcoming events immediately, or change the section to show "Past Highlights" with a clear label. Consider adding a "No upcoming events — check back soon!" message with a newsletter signup.
High
09
💡 About Section Is a Wall of Text with No Visual Hierarchy
The "Get to Know Us Better" section is a single large paragraph of text with no subheadings, bullet points, icons, or visual breaks. This makes it very difficult to read, especially on mobile, and most users will skip it entirely.
Break the About text into clearly labelled subsections: Mission, Certifications (with badge icons), Key Projects (bullet list), and a "Learn More" button linking to the full About page.
Medium
10
💡 No Testimonials or Beneficiary Stories
The website has no quotes, testimonials, or impact stories from volunteers, beneficiaries, or community members. These are the most emotionally compelling content for an NGO and significantly improve donor/volunteer conversion rates.
Add a "Voices of Change" section with 3–5 short testimonials from volunteers or community members, with their photo, name, and role.
Medium
11
💡 No Donate Progress Bar or Fundraising Transparency
The website has a "Donate Us" button but no visible fundraising goals, progress bars, or transparency on how funds are used. Studies show NGOs with transparent fund usage metrics receive significantly more donations.
Add a donation progress bar for active campaigns (e.g., "₹35,000 raised of ₹1,00,000 goal for Bachpanshala"). Include a brief fund allocation breakdown (e.g., 80% programmes, 20% operations).
Medium
12
💡 No Mobile Navigation Menu (Hamburger Menu)
On mobile devices, the navigation links are not accessible via a hamburger menu, which means mobile users (likely the majority of visitors) cannot navigate the site easily. This is a critical UX gap.
Implement a responsive hamburger menu that opens a slide-in navigation drawer on screens below 768px width. This is standard mobile UX practice.
Medium
13
✨ Overall Visual Design Feels Generic / Template-like
The current website uses a generic CMS template with no distinctive brand identity, custom colour palette, or memorable typography. The design does not reflect the warmth, passion, and energy of the InAmigos community.
Define a brand colour palette (e.g., saffron, forest green, cream) and apply it consistently. Use a distinctive display font for headings. Add custom illustrations or icons for each project.
Low
14
✨ No Newsletter / Email Signup to Build Community
There is no email newsletter signup on the website. Building an email list is one of the most valuable long-term assets for an NGO — it enables direct communication with supporters, donors, and volunteers.
Add a simple email signup section: "Stay updated on our work — join 500+ supporters" with a name + email field and a subscribe button connected to Mailchimp or a similar free service.
Low

Conclusion & Next Steps

InAmigos Foundation has a strong mission, real social impact, and a growing community — but the current website does not reflect this energy. Fixing the critical security issues first, then improving the design and content, will dramatically increase trust, volunteer sign-ups, and donor conversions.
